Reintroduction of exposed/unexposed frogs: summary

This notebook provides a description of all frog reintroductions conducted to date that included both Bd-exposed and unexposed (control) frogs.

Spur Basin to Upper Basin/Pinchot Basin reintroduction

  • On 2013-07-01, we collected adult R. muscosa from three lakes in Spur Basin and transported them out of the backcountry via helicopter. Frogs at all collection locations were Bd-naive. Frogs were transferred to SNARL, divided into exposed and unexposed groups, and held in indoor tanks. 

  • On 2013-07-20, Mary conducted the first Bd exposure of the frogs in the exposed group. [Details regarding Bd strains used, inoculation protocol, etc.?] All frogs were swabbed on day 5, 10, and 14 post-exposure. Exposed frogs became infected but had low loads on all swabbing dates. 

  • On 2013-07-21, all frogs [or exposed frogs only?] were treated with itraconazole for seven days. [Details of treatment protocol?] Swabs collected on 2013-07-27 indicated that all frogs were Bd-negative.

  • On 2013-07-30, Mary conducted the second Bd exposure of the frogs in the exposed group, and swabs were collected from all frogs on day 4 and day 11 post-exposure. Frogs had low loads on day 4 and were all Bd-negative on day 11.

  • Reasons for the low loads following both exposures were unknown, but one possibility is that conditions at SNARL (e.g., water chemistry, etc.) were not ideal for Bd growth. Therefore, frogs were transferred to UCSB prior to a third attempt at infecting frogs.

  • On 2013-08-17, Mary conducted the third Bd exposure of frogs in the exposed group. All frogs were swabbed on days 5 and 10. Bd loads were moderate on day 5 and low or zero on day 10.

  • With no obvious path forward or means of maintaining frogs overwinter at UCSB, frogs were cleared of Bd with itracozole [dates?] and prepared for reintroduction to three lakes in Upper and Pinchot Basins.

  • On 2013-09-04, exposed and unexposed frogs were flow by helicopter into the Kings Canyon National Park and reintroduced to three lakes (10277, 10285, 10315). Populations were monitored for at least two summers using CMR surveys. Within three years, frogs in all three populations developed high Bd loads and declined to extirpation or near-extirpation.
